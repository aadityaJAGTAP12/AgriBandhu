Fertilizer Guidelines for Indian Agriculture

Balanced fertilization is essential for sustainable crop production and soil health maintenance.

SOIL TESTING
- Test soil before every cropping season
- Parameters: pH, organic carbon, available NPK, micronutrients
- Use government soil testing labs or private services
- Soil health card scheme provides recommendations

MACRONUTRIENTS

NITROGEN (N)
- Essential for vegetative growth and chlorophyll formation
- Sources: Urea (46% N), Ammonium sulfate (21% N), CAN (25% N)
- Deficiency symptoms: Yellowing of older leaves, stunted growth
- Excess: Lodging, delayed maturity, poor quality

PHOSPHORUS (P)
- Important for root development and energy transfer
- Sources: DAP (18% N, 46% P2O5), SSP (16% P2O5), Rock phosphate
- Deficiency: Purple coloration on leaves, poor root growth
- Applied as basal dose for most crops

POTASSIUM (K)
- Regulates water balance and disease resistance
- Sources: MOP (60% K2O), SOP (50% K2O)
- Deficiency: Scorching of leaf margins, weak stems
- Important for fruit quality and stress tolerance

FERTILIZER RECOMMENDATIONS BY CROP

RICE
- N:P:K = 120:60:40 kg/ha
- Apply 50% N + full P,K basal
- Remaining N at tillering and panicle initiation
- Avoid excess N in heavy soils

WHEAT
- N:P:K = 120:60:40 kg/ha
- Split N application: 50% basal, 25% at CRI, 25% at jointing
- Full P,K as basal
- Zinc sulfate @ 25 kg/ha if deficient

COTTON
- N:P:K = 120:60:60 kg/ha
- Apply in 3-4 splits
- Boron @ 1 kg/ha for boll development
- Avoid chloride-containing fertilizers

MAIZE
- N:P:K = 150:75:75 kg/ha
- Heavy N feeder, apply in splits
- Zinc and iron often deficient
- Use chelated micronutrients

SUGARCANE
- N:P:K = 250:100:250 kg/ha
- Apply in 5-6 splits throughout growth
- High K requirement for sugar accumulation
- Organic manure essential

MICRONUTRIENTS

ZINC (Zn)
- Most common deficiency in Indian soils
- Sources: Zinc sulfate, zinc oxide
- Apply 25 kg/ha as basal or foliar spray
- Deficiency: White buds, stunted growth

IRON (Fe)
- Deficiency in alkaline soils
- Sources: Ferrous sulfate, Fe-EDTA
- Foliar application more effective
- Deficiency: Interveinal chlorosis

BORON (B)
- Important for reproductive development
- Sources: Borax, solubor
- Apply 1-2 kg/ha
- Deficiency: Cracked stems, poor pollination

MANGANESE (Mn)
- Required in small quantities
- Sources: Manganese sulfate
- Deficiency: Grey spots on leaves
- More common in alkaline soils

FERTILIZER APPLICATION METHODS

BASAL APPLICATION
- Mix fertilizers with soil before sowing/transplanting
- Place fertilizers 5-10 cm below and to the side of seeds
- Avoid direct contact with seeds

TOP DRESSING
- Apply fertilizers to soil surface after crop establishment
- Irrigate immediately after application
- Split applications prevent losses

FOLIAR APPLICATION
- For micronutrients and quick correction
- Use 0.5-1% concentration
- Apply in evening or early morning
- Effective for Zn, Fe, Mn deficiencies

FERTIGATION
- Application through irrigation water
- Requires drip/sprinkler systems
- Precise nutrient delivery
- Reduces fertilizer losses

ORGANIC FERTILIZERS

FARMYARD MANURE (FYM)
- 10-15 tonnes/ha for most crops
- Improves soil structure and water holding capacity
- Slow release of nutrients
- Suppresses soil-borne diseases

VERMICOMPOST
- 2-3 tonnes/ha
- Rich in beneficial microbes
- Better nutrient availability
- Improves soil aeration

BIOFERTILIZERS
- Azospirillum for nitrogen fixation
- Phosphate solubilizing bacteria
- Zinc solubilizing bacteria
- Apply as seed treatment or soil application

INTEGRATED NUTRIENT MANAGEMENT
- Combine chemical fertilizers with organic sources
- Use biofertilizers for sustainable production
- Site-specific nutrient management
- Regular soil testing and monitoring
