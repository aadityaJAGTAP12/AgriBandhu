Monsoon Farming Guidelines for Indian Agriculture

Monsoon season (June-September) is critical for kharif crops and overall agricultural productivity.

MONSOON CHARACTERISTICS

RAINFALL PATTERNS
- Southwest monsoon: June-September
- Average rainfall: 750-1500 mm
- Variability: 20-50% year-to-year
- Distribution: Uneven spatial and temporal
- Extreme events: Droughts and floods

CLIMATE VARIABILITY
- El Niño/La Niña effects on monsoon
- Climate change impacts
- Increasing extreme weather events
- Temperature rise affecting crop growth
- Changing pest and disease patterns

CROP PLANNING FOR MONSOON

KHARIF CROP SELECTION
- Rice: Main monsoon crop, requires 150-200 cm water
- Maize: Drought-tolerant, good for variable rainfall
- Cotton: Commercial crop, moderate water requirement
- Soybean: Short duration, fits in monsoon
- Groundnut: Drought-resistant, fits variable conditions

SEED AND VARIETY CHOICE
- Short-duration varieties for delayed monsoon
- Drought-tolerant varieties for dry areas
- Flood-tolerant varieties for low-lying areas
- Disease-resistant varieties
- Climate-resilient varieties

LAND PREPARATION

TIMELY PREPARATION
- Complete ploughing before monsoon onset
- Field leveling for uniform water distribution
- Drainage channels for excess water
- Bunds and ridges for water conservation
- Weed control before sowing

SOIL MOISTURE MANAGEMENT
- Utilize pre-monsoon showers
- Sow immediately after first good rains
- Avoid waterlogging in heavy soils
- Improve drainage in problem areas
- Conserve soil moisture

SOWING STRATEGIES

OPTIMUM SOWING WINDOWS
- Rice: June 15-July 15
- Maize: June 15-July 15
- Cotton: May 15-June 15
- Soybean: June 15-July 15
- Groundnut: June 15-July 15

CONTINGENCY PLANNING
