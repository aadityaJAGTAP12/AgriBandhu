Pest Control Guidelines for Indian Agriculture

Integrated Pest Management (IPM) is essential for sustainable agriculture and environmental protection.

INTEGRATED PEST MANAGEMENT (IPM)

PRINCIPLES OF IPM
- Prevention through resistant varieties and crop rotation
- Monitoring pest populations and damage
- Economic threshold determination
- Biological control methods
- Chemical control as last resort
- Regular evaluation of pest management practices

PEST MONITORING
- Regular field scouting (weekly during critical stages)
- Use of pheromone traps for insect pests
- Yellow/blue sticky traps for monitoring
- Light traps for nocturnal insects
- Economic threshold levels (ETL) for decision making

BIOLOGICAL CONTROL
- Natural enemies: Ladybird beetles, spiders, predatory mites
- Parasitic wasps and flies
- Entomopathogenic fungi and bacteria
- Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt) for caterpillar pests
- Trichoderma for fungal diseases

CULTURAL CONTROL
- Crop rotation to break pest cycles
- Proper field sanitation and residue management
- Optimum plant spacing for air circulation
- Timely sowing and harvesting
- Resistant/tolerant varieties

MECHANICAL CONTROL
- Hand picking of large insects
- Use of light traps and pheromone traps
- Bird perches for insect control
- Row covers and netting
- Soil solarization

CHEMICAL CONTROL

INSECTICIDES
- Systemic insecticides: Imidacloprid, Thiamethoxam
- Contact insecticides: Cypermethrin, Lambda cyhalothrin
- Stomach poisons: Carbaryl, Malathion
- Selective insecticides: Spinosad, Neem oil

FUNGICIDES
- Contact fungicides: Copper oxychloride, Mancozeb
- Systemic fungicides: Carbendazim, Hexaconazole
- Preventive fungicides: Bordeaux mixture
- Biofungicides: Trichoderma, Pseudomonas

ACARICIDES
- Sulfur-based: Wettable sulfur
- Organophosphates: Dimethoate
- Modern: Abamectin, Fenazaquin

PEST MANAGEMENT BY CROP

RICE
- Stem borer: Release Trichogramma wasps, apply cartap hydrochloride
- Brown plant hopper: Use resistant varieties, apply buprofezin
- Leaf folder: Neem oil sprays, release natural enemies
- Blast disease: Use resistant varieties, apply tricyclazole

WHEAT
- Aphids: Release ladybird beetles, apply imidacloprid
- Armyworm: Bacillus thuringiensis, chlorpyrifos
- Rust diseases: Use resistant varieties, apply propiconazole
- Loose smut: Seed treatment with carbendazim

COTTON
- Bollworm: Bt cotton varieties, NPV virus
- Jassids: Apply imidacloprid or thiamethoxam
- Whitefly: Release Encarsia wasps, neem oil
- Bacterial blight: Copper-based fungicides

MAIZE
- Stem borer: Release Trichogramma, apply emamectin benzoate
- Armyworm: Bt sprays, spinosad
- Downy mildew: Metalaxyl + mancozeb
- Rust: Propiconazole sprays

SUGARCANE
- Early shoot borer: Cartap hydrochloride
- Top borer: Release Trichogramma chilonis
- Pyrilla: Neem oil, azadirachtin
- Red rot: Resistant varieties, hot water treatment of setts

SAFETY PRECAUTIONS
- Use personal protective equipment (PPE)
- Follow label instructions and dosage
- Avoid spraying during windy conditions
- Respect waiting periods before harvest
- Proper storage and disposal of pesticides

RESISTANCE MANAGEMENT
- Rotate different groups of pesticides
- Use recommended doses only
- Avoid frequent use of same pesticide
- Combine chemical with biological control
- Monitor for resistance development

ENVIRONMENTAL CONSIDERATIONS
- Avoid pesticide drift to water bodies
- Protect beneficial insects and pollinators
- Use selective pesticides
- Promote organic farming practices
- Follow integrated approaches

TRAINING AND EXTENSION
- Regular training programs for farmers
- Demonstration plots for new technologies
- Farmer field schools for IPM
- Access to pest forecasting services
- Government extension services
