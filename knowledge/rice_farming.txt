Rice Farming Guide - India

Rice (Oryza sativa) is the staple food crop in India, grown in both kharif and rabi seasons.

LAND PREPARATION
- Deep ploughing during summer to expose soil to sun
- Level the field properly for uniform water distribution
- Apply basal fertilizer (NPK) before transplanting

SEED SELECTION AND SOWING
- Use certified seeds of high-yielding varieties like Swarna, MTU-1010
- Nursery preparation: 20-25 days old seedlings
- Transplanting: 20-25 cm row spacing, 15-20 cm plant spacing
- Optimum sowing time: June-July for kharif, December-January for rabi

WATER MANAGEMENT
- Maintain 5-7 cm water level during vegetative stage
- Reduce water during reproductive stage to prevent lodging
- Total water requirement: 1200-1500 mm per season
- Avoid water stress during flowering and grain filling

FERTILIZER APPLICATION
- N:P:K ratio 120:60:40 kg/ha
- Apply 50% N and full P,K as basal
- Remaining N in 2-3 splits at tillering and panicle initiation
- Use organic manure (10-15 tonnes/ha) for soil health

PEST AND DISEASE MANAGEMENT
- Common pests: Stem borer, leaf folder, brown plant hopper
- Diseases: Blast, bacterial blight, sheath blight
- Use integrated pest management (IPM)
- Apply pesticides only when economic threshold reached
- Encourage natural enemies and use resistant varieties

WEED CONTROL
- First weeding 15-20 days after transplanting
- Second weeding 35-40 days after transplanting
- Use pre-emergence herbicides like Butachlor
- Manual weeding for organic farming

HARVESTING
- Harvest when 80-85% grains are straw colored
- Use combine harvester for large fields
- Dry grains to 12-14% moisture content
- Store in clean, dry bins

YIELD EXPECTATIONS
- Average yield: 4-6 tonnes/ha
- High-yielding varieties: 6-8 tonnes/ha
- Factors affecting yield: Variety, soil fertility, water management, pest control

CLIMATE REQUIREMENTS
- Temperature: 20-35°C optimum
- Rainfall: 150-200 cm annually
- Soil: Clay loam to clay, pH 5.5-6.5
- Avoid waterlogging and drought stress

MONSOON CONSIDERATIONS
- Ensure proper drainage during heavy rains
- Monitor for pest outbreaks during humid conditions
- Apply fertilizers before heavy rains to prevent leaching
- Harvest before monsoon if crop is ready
