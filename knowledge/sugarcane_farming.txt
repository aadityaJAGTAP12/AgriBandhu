Sugarcane Farming Guide - India

Sugarcane (Saccharum officinarum) is a long-duration commercial crop grown for sugar production.

LAND PREPARATION
- Deep ploughing (45-60 cm) to break hard pans
- Apply trash mulching for soil moisture conservation
- Form ridges and furrows for irrigation
- Remove perennial weeds and crop residues

SEED MATERIAL AND PLANTING
- Use healthy 8-10 month old setts
- Seed rate: 35,000-40,000 setts/ha (3-budded)
- Planting time: February-March for autumn planting
- Spacing: 90x30 cm or 75x45 cm
- Planting depth: 5-7 cm

IRRIGATION MANAGEMENT
- First irrigation: Immediately after planting
- Subsequent irrigations: Every 7-10 days
- Critical stages: Tillering, grand growth, maturity
- Maintain soil moisture at field capacity
- Total water requirement: 2000-2500 mm

FERTILIZER RECOMMENDATIONS
- N:P:K ratio 250:100:250 kg/ha
- Apply 25% N,P,K as basal
- Remaining fertilizers in 4-5 splits
- Apply micronutrients (Zn, Fe, Mn) as needed
- Use organic manure @ 20-25 tonnes/ha

PEST AND DISEASE CONTROL
- Major pests: Early shoot borer, top borer, pyrilla
- Diseases: Red rot, smut, wilt
- Use disease-free seed material
- Apply systemic insecticides preventively
- Practice proper field sanitation

WEED MANAGEMENT
- Pre-emergence herbicides: Atrazine + Metribuzin
- Manual weeding 30-45 days after planting
- Inter-cultivation to control weeds
- Mulching with trash for weed suppression

CROP MANAGEMENT
- Gap filling within 15-20 days of planting
- Earthing up at 90-120 days
- Propping to prevent lodging
- Detrashing for better growth

HARVESTING
- Harvest when sucrose content is maximum (18-20 months)
- Use cane cutters or mechanical harvesters
- Transport immediately to sugar mills
- Avoid delays to prevent sucrose loss

YIELD EXPECTATIONS
- Average cane yield: 80-100 tonnes/ha
- Sugar recovery: 9-11%
- Commercial cane sugar: 8-10 tonnes/ha

CLIMATE REQUIREMENTS
- Temperature: 20-35°C optimum
- Rainfall: 150-200 cm well distributed
- Soil: Deep, fertile, well-drained alluvial soils
- pH: 6.5-7.5

ECONOMIC CONSIDERATIONS
- High establishment cost but long-term returns
- Proper management crucial for profitability
- Irrigation and nutrient management key factors
- Disease management prevents yield losses
