Wheat Farming Guide - India

Wheat (Triticum aestivum) is the second most important cereal crop in India after rice.

LAND PREPARATION
- Plough 4-5 times to get fine tilth
- Apply farmyard manure during last ploughing
- Level the field for uniform irrigation
- Remove weeds and crop residues

SEED SELECTION AND SOWING
- Use certified seeds of varieties like HD-2967, PBW-725
- Seed rate: 100-125 kg/ha for normal sowing, 125-150 kg/ha for late sowing
- Sowing depth: 4-5 cm
- Row spacing: 20-22 cm
- Optimum sowing time: November-December (rabi season)

IRRIGATION MANAGEMENT
- First irrigation: Immediately after sowing (crown root initiation)
- Second irrigation: At tillering stage (21-25 days)
- Third irrigation: At jointing stage (45-50 days)
- Fourth irrigation: At flowering stage (65-70 days)
- Fifth irrigation: At grain filling stage (85-90 days)
- Total water requirement: 450-500 mm

FERTILIZER RECOMMENDATIONS
- N:P:K ratio 120:60:40 kg/ha
- Apply full P and K, and 50% N as basal
- Remaining N in two splits: At first and third irrigation
- Zinc sulfate @ 25 kg/ha if deficiency observed
- Avoid excess nitrogen to prevent lodging

PEST AND DISEASE CONTROL
- Common pests: Aphids, termites, armyworm
- Diseases: Rust, powdery mildew, loose smut
- Use seed treatment with fungicides
- Spray insecticides at economic threshold
- Practice crop rotation to reduce disease incidence

WEED MANAGEMENT
- First hoeing: 20-25 days after sowing
- Second hoeing: 40-45 days after sowing
- Use post-emergence herbicides like Sulfosulfuron
- Manual weeding for organic cultivation

HARVESTING AND STORAGE
- Harvest when moisture content is 12-14%
- Use combine harvester for efficiency
- Dry grains properly before storage
- Store in moisture-proof containers
- Protect from rodents and insects

YIELD POTENTIAL
- Average yield: 4-5 tonnes/ha
- High-yielding varieties: 5-6 tonnes/ha
- Record yields: 7-8 tonnes/ha with good management

CLIMATE REQUIREMENTS
- Temperature: 15-25°C during growth, cooler during grain filling
- Rainfall: 75-100 cm annually, mostly during rabi
- Soil: Well-drained loamy soil, pH 6.0-7.5
- Avoid frost damage during flowering

WINTER MANAGEMENT
- Protect from frost during early growth
- Ensure proper drainage to prevent waterlogging
- Monitor for cold weather pests
- Apply protective irrigation during cold spells
